Project Implementation Plan

A Phased Approach for Scalable Biogas Plant Construction

Our Two-Phase Strategy

This plan is divided into two phases. First, we establish a generic foundation (Parenting) (blue steps) to create a standardized template. This foundation is then used to execute the full plan (all steps) for each individual biogas plant, ensuring consistency and efficiency across all locations.

Sites: Sjöbo, Hörby, Kalmar, Vormstad

Foundation Step 1

Equipment Placement

Plan the placement of all equipment in the Solidbuilding and pump house to finalize building size, layout, and ensure serviceability.

Tasks:

  • Inventory all equipment
  • Develop a 2D layout
  • Analyze for serviceability
  • Determine final building size
  • Finalize locations for doors and rooms and truck logistics

Goal:

To position all equipment correctly, ensure it is easy to service, and finalize a building design that supports efficient operations.

Foundation Step 2

Electrical Equipment Placement

Strategically place major electrical equipment like transformers and switchgear to ensure safety, ventilation, and reduce cabling costs.

Tasks:

  • List all major electrical equipment
  • Develop a preliminary layout
  • Review for compliance and safety
  • Finalize electrical room designs

Goal:

To strategically place all electrical equipment for safety, serviceability, and efficiency, and to finalize the design of electrical rooms.

Foundation Step 3

Internal Piping Layout

Plan the layout for main internal pipes, such as biomass lines and ventilation, to simplify outdoor connections and ensure flexibility.

Tasks:

  • Identify all major pipes and ducts
  • Design the layout and interface points
  • Review for flexibility and safety

Goal:

To design a flexible "building block" piping system with clear interface points, simplifying outdoor connections and ensuring safety.

Site-Specific Step 4

Site Piping System

Design the site piping system connecting buildings and tanks, comparing underground vs. above-ground installation costs and benefits.

Tasks:

  • Analyze piping options
  • Select the best layout
  • Design main pipeline routes (WP6)
  • Prepare 2D drawings

Goal:

To select the most cost-effective and practical piping layout and finalize the design for the main connecting pipelines.

Foundation Step 5

Internal Cable Routes

Design the main routes for electrical and instrumentation cables inside buildings to prevent conflicts with other systems.

Tasks:

  • Map primary cable pathways
  • Design layout for cable trays
  • Verify the plan is clash-free and safe

Goal:

To design a clash-free and efficient internal cable routing system that is safe, easy to install, and allows for future maintenance.

Site-Specific Step 6

External Cable Routes

Design site-wide cable routes connecting all buildings, comparing routing options and evaluating cost-saving technologies like distributed I/O.

Tasks:

  • Analyze routing options
  • Evaluate technology options
  • Finalize the site-wide cable plan

Goal:

To select the most cost-effective cabling strategy and finalize the design of the main routes connecting all facilities.

Foundation Step 7

Workshop and Storage Facility

Determine the best design and location for the workshop and storage facilities, deciding whether to attach them to an existing building or combine them with the Service House.

Tasks:

  • Define operational needs and space requirements
  • Develop conceptual layouts for each option
  • Establish decision criteria (cost, workflow)
  • Select and place the preferred option
  • Prepare a preliminary scope

Goal:

To decide on the best configuration and location for the workshop and storage facilities and create a template plan for future projects.
